## SEQUENCE BEST PRACTICES

### Send Timing

| Email | Best Day | Best Time (Local to Recipient) | Why |
|-------|----------|-------------------------------|-----|
| Email 1 | Tuesday or Wednesday | 8:30–9:15 AM | GMs/Directors check email first thing; before the lot gets busy |
| Email 2 | Friday | 8:00–9:00 AM | Decision-makers often in strategic headspace Friday AM; slower deal floor |
| Email 3 | Tuesday (following week) | 9:00–10:00 AM | Consistency with initial send day; avoids Monday chaos |

**Avoid:** Monday mornings (high volume, low attention), Friday afternoons, weekends.

---

### Subject Line Notes

- **Personalization matters:** Subject lines with `[DEALERSHIP_NAME]` or `[FIRST_NAME]` will outperform generic on cold lists. Always verify merge tags are populated before send.
- **A/B test one element at a time:** Test the variant you believe is strongest against one alternative per email. Don't split 3 ways on a small list.
- **Email 1 subject** should be the most pattern-interrupt. This is where you win or lose the sequence.
- **Avoid:** Spam trigger words ("FREE", "Act now", "Limited time", excessive caps/exclamation points)
- **Email 3 subject** is your most powerful open driver by psychological effect — "closing the loop" emails routinely see 2–3x open rates of touch 1. Use it wisely.

---

### Follow-Up Timing Rationale

| Gap | Reason |
|-----|--------|
| Email 1 → Email 2: 3 days | Short enough to stay top-of-mind; long enough to avoid feeling pushy. A 3-day gap implies confidence, not desperation. |
| Email 2 → Email 3: 4 days | Slightly longer signals you're giving them space. The breakup email lands better when it doesn't feel rushed. |
| Post-sequence: 30–60 days | If no reply after Email 3, re-enter with a new angle (news hook, new stat, product update). Keep contact warm — these are long-cycle B2B prospects. |

---

### Additional Sequence Notes

**On personalization:**
- If you have a specific insight about the dealership (recent awards, new inventory, expansion news), insert a 1-sentence personal reference in Email 1 — between the opener and the V·GUIDE introduction. This dramatically increases reply rates.
- Example: *"I noticed [DEALERSHIP_NAME] was recently recognized as a top volume dealer in [CITY] — congrats on that. That kind of lead volume makes the problem I'm about to describe even more relevant."*

**On deliverability:**
- Send from your own domain (ethan@voxaris.io) — not a bulk ESP for the first 21 prospects. Personal sending = personal feel.
- Warm the domain if sending in volume. 5–10 emails/day max from a new domain.
- Plain-text formatting preferred for cold outreach. No images, no HTML-heavy templates. Looks like a real email from a real person.

**On call booking:**
- Link `voxaris.io/book-demo` in every email, but also offer to reply and schedule directly. Some prospects prefer the informal reply.
- 15-minute frame is intentional — lower friction than "30-minute call." These are busy GMs.

**On the 21-prospect list:**
- With 21 contacts, this is personal outreach — treat it as such. Review each contact before sending. Customize the opening line of Email 1 where possible.
- Track opens/clicks in a lightweight CRM or Google Sheet. Any dealership that opens Email 1 twice without replying is a priority call prospect.
